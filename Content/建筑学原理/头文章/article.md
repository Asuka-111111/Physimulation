# Particle Movement in Magnetic Field

> **Category:** Overview | **Words:** ~850  
> **Cover:** ![Cover](cover.jpg)

---

In the magnetic field, a charged particle will experience an external force called the *Lorentz force*, which will alter its trajectory into circular motion.
The function of the Lorentz force is
$$
\vec{F} = q (\vec{v} \times \vec{B})
$$
Assuming there is a constant uniform magnetic field $  \vec{B} = B \hat{z}  $, the equation of motion is derived as: $m \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt} = q (\vec{v} \times \vec{B})$.
In component form, 

Therefore, The particle will move at constant velocity parallel to the magnetic field in *z* direction. 


The cyclotron frequency can  be calculated as: $  \omega = \frac{q B}{m}  $
